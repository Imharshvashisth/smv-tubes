Plants & Machinery

Step inside our state-of-the-art manufacturing facility. Raw steel is forged into architectural masterpieces using heavy-duty, world-class precision machinery.

Manufacturing Arsenal

Heavy-Duty Capabilities

01
Precision Forming

High-Frequency Tube Mills

Our automated tube mills utilize high-frequency induction welding to ensure flawless, continuous seams. Capable of handling massive gauges and diameters with zero tolerance for error.

  • Diameter Capacity: 10mm to 350mm
  • High-Speed Production Lines
  • PLC Controlled Precision Automation
02
Thermal Dynamics

Continuous Bright Annealing

To relieve internal stresses generated during the welding process, tubes are passed through our state-of-the-art bright annealing furnaces in a controlled hydrogen atmosphere.

  • Temperature Control up to 1150°C
  • Prevents Surface Oxidation
  • Enhances Tensile Ductility
03
Flawless Finish

Multi-Head Polishing Lines

Aesthetics matter. Our advanced multi-head polishing machines deliver a mirror-like finish, essential for architectural and pharmaceutical grade stainless steel applications.

  • Grit Finish: 180G, 320G, 400G, 600G
  • Mirror & Matte Finishes Available
  • Enhances Anti-Corrosive Properties
04
Raw Material Prep

Precision Slitting Lines

The journey begins here. Our heavy-duty slitting lines cut massive stainless steel master coils into exact widths required for specific tube dimensions with zero burr tolerance.

  • High Precision Rotary Knives
  • Handles Master Coils up to 25 Tons
  • Exact Width Tolerance (± 0.05mm)
Quality Assurance

The Testing Laboratory

Every single batch is subjected to rigorous destructive and non-destructive testing before it leaves our facility.

Spectro Analysis

Instant computerized chemical composition testing to verify the exact grade of stainless steel (304, 316L, etc.) using OES technology.

Universal Testing Machine

Evaluates the mechanical properties including Tensile Strength, Yield Strength, and Elongation of the finished tubes.

Hydrostatic Testing

Tubes are filled with water and pressurized to ensure there are no microscopic leaks or weld failures under extreme stress.

Metallography Check

Microscopic examination to inspect the grain structure of the weld seam to ensure proper fusion and homogeneity.

Flaring & Flattening

Destructive testing methods to check the ductility of the material and the integrity of the welded joint.

Surface & Dimension Check

Digital verniers, micrometers, and surface roughness testers ensure physical specs match international standards.
